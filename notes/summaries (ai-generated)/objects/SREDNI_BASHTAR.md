# SREDNI BASHTAR

A short story by SAKI (pseudonym of Hector Hugh Monroe), published 1911. Mysteriously delivered to all three agents on the same day in 2004 via animals, three years after the CORNUCOPIA HOUSE operation.

## The Deliveries (2004)

From Session 3:

> events:
>   - on the same day in 2004, three people smell ROTTING FLESH: (THREES)
>     - LOTTIE: at grocery store a few years later, follows a blood trail into th eback room where there's a dying bat on top of an item (ANIMALS, THREES)
>     - ERASTUS: driving truck home in the dark, then hits a deer; cops find an item on the car (ANIMALS, THREES)
>     - SEAMUS: waiting for daughter at sleepover; daughter gets home holding an item which she says was given by a crow (ANIMALS, THREES)
>   - in all three cases, the item is a short story, very short book, titled SREDNI BASHTAR by SAKI, pseudonym of Hector Hugh Monroe, published 1911 (ANIMALS, THREES)

## Thematic Connections

- All three deliveries involved ANIMALS (bat, deer, crow) - connecting to the ANIMALS theme
- All three deliveries occurred on the same day in 2004 - connecting to the THREES theme
- Each agent smelled ROTTING FLESH when receiving the story
- The deliveries occurred exactly 3 years after the 2001 CORNUCOPIA HOUSE operation

## Notes

- The story title "SREDNI BASHTAR" may be significant to the cult's beliefs or practices
- The coordinated delivery via dying/dead animals suggests supernatural orchestration
- SAKI was a British writer known for dark, satirical short stories
- The story may contain coded information or serve as a ritual marker
- The fact that it was delivered to all three agents suggests they are still being tracked/targeted by the cult
